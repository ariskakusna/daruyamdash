What does it sound like when a rod bearing fails?

When a rod bearing fails, the engine will make a metallic banging or rumbling noise, which will be particularly noticeable during cold starts and acceleration. This is referred to as ‘rod knock,’ and it produces a sound similar to that of hitting a tin or aluminium surface. The degree of knocking is minimal at idle and rises with acceleration.
