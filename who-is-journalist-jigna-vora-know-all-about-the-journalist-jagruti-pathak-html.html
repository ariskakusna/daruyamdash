Let’s dive into the intriguing world of journalism and get to know a remarkable individual – Journalist Jigna Vora. You might have stumbled upon her name with the buzz around the Hindi series Scoop, based on her biographical memoir Behind Bars in Byculla: My Days in Prison. So, who exactly is Journalist Jigna Vora, and what’s her story? | Well, grab a comfy seat, and let’s share news about it.

Who is Journalist Jigna Vora?

Jigna Vora, a name echoing in the journalistic corridors, started her journey as a crime reporter for the Asian Age newspaper in the vibrant city of Mumbai. Despite holding a law degree from Ruparel College and a diploma from Somaiya College, her path wasn’t always lined with the ink of a reporter’s pen. In an unexpected twist, parental pressure led her down the aisle with a self-proclaimed engineer from Baruch, Gujarat.

Spoiler alert: turns out, her marriage was built on false claims, and it hit rock bottom. In 2004, Jigna, with her four-year-old son in tow, decided to hit the reset button. Back at her maternal home in Ghatkopar, Mumbai, she embarked on a new journey – this time, chasing her passion for the media.

Journalist Jigna Vora Wiki

Fast forward to today, Jigna Vora has become a senior reporter for the BBC News Hindi Service, with a decade-long stint at the BBC. Her reporting résumé reads like a gripping novel – covering stories ranging from the 2008 Mumbai terror attacks to the Gujarat riots and the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic. A proud alumna of the Indian Institute of Mass Communication and the University of Mumbai, she has bagged prestigious awards, including the Ramnath Goenka Award for Excellence in Journalism and the Chameli Devi Jain Award for Outstanding Woman Journalist. But Jigna is not just a journalist; she’s a force for human rights and social justice. Her commitment extends to memberships in the International Women’s Media Foundation and the South Asian Journalists Association.

Jigna Vora Husband

Now, let’s spill the beans on a chapter that added a dash of controversy to Jigna’s journey. In 2011, a storm hit her career when she faced accusations of conspiring with gangster Chhota Rajan in the murder of veteran crime reporter J Dey. But rewind a bit – her personal life had its fair share of twists. Succumbing to parental pressure, she entered a marriage that turned out to be a mirage. False claims shattered the union, leading her back to Mumbai in 200Divorce marked a turning point, ushering in emotional challenges and changes in Jigna’s life. As she moved forward, she embraced single parenthood, raising her son from her previous marriage to Suresh. Juggling the demands of being a single parent and a journalist is no easy feat, but Jigna’s dedication shines through.

Jigna Vora Son

Meet the young co-pilot in Jigna’s life – her son. While specific details about Jigna’s children remain undisclosed, it’s known that she’s navigating the challenges of single motherhood with grace. Being a journalist and a single mom requires a delicate balance, ensuring her child’s well-being while pursuing her professional passion. Jigna’s decision to shift from Gujarat to Mumbai wasn’t just a career move; it was a step towards providing her child with better opportunities and a brighter future. It’s a testament to her commitment to creating an environment where her child can thrive and succeed. And there you have it – the tale of Journalist Jigna Vora, a woman who faced twists, turns, and controversies but emerged as a resilient force in the world of journalism. Here’s to Jigna, her journey, and the stories she continues to unfold. Cheers to the unsung heroes behind the headlines!
