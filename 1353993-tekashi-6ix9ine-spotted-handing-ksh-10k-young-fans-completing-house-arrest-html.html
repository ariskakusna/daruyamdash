Tekashi 6ix9ine spotted handing out N40k each to young fans after completing house arrest

Posted by Tandra Barner on Wednesday, August 21, 2024

- The controversial rapper stunned many online after handing out $100 bills (N38,853) to young kids in the streets of New York

- The kids were definitely overjoyed as they appreciated the rapper for his generosity

- Tekashi has been spending more time outdoors ever since the conclusion of his house arrest

- This, however, would alert many as the rapper is expected to keep a low profile due to cases of snitching on his gang

US controversial rapper Tekashi 6ix9ine recently ended his house arrest term and is already enjoying his long-awaited freedom.

The Gooba hitmaker has been running around the streets posing with fans and even playing football at a New York park with his beloved fans.

But what has left many stunned is a video of him handing out $100 bills (N38,853) to young kids in the streets.

The video was shared on his Instagram account and one could spot the rapper dishing out cash to New York kids who were happy to ask for more.

Older fans even came along and were also handed the cash which was obviously in abundance.

"Get some money men,' the rapper could be heard urging his fans."

The kids were definitely overjoyed as they appreciated the rapper for his generosity and even one could be heard saying: "Tekashi is damn rich."

The rapper was recently released from jail after his lawyers begged for an early release with fears that he could contract COVID-19 in jail.

Just a few days after his release, Tekashi went on social media sharing pictures while flaunting his riches on a balcony at a new crib.

Like he did not know better, the ex-con exposed too much of his new place that many could notice and alert others.

Someone apparently snitched on him (the irony) revealing the house by posting the photo while also sharing the address.

The musician's lawyer, Lance Lazzaro, later said his client had been relocated due to the leak for security reasons and the cops were made aware of it.

Tekashi was not violating house arrest rules as he had been exposed and considering his enemies were probably out to get him it, it was crucial that he moved.

The court had ordered him to lay low and avoid bringing too much attention to himself but as it seems, he can not do without it.
