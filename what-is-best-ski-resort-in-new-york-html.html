What is The Best Ski Resort In New York

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, August 4, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

The New York Ski Hill Showdown: Finding Your Perfect Peak (Without the Wipeout)

Ah, New York. The city that never sleeps... unless you're face-planted in glorious powder after epic ski run. But with more ski resorts than you can shake a ski pole at, choosing the right one can be trickier than navigating rush hour traffic on a single ski. Fear not, intrepid snow bunnies and gnarly dudes, for this guide will cut through the slush and help you find your perfect New York ski resort.

The All-Star Mountain:

Gore Mountain: This Adirondack champion boasts the most vertical drop in the state (over 2,500 feet!), stunning panoramic views, and a relaxed vibe that's easier on the wallet than therapy after a brutal wipeout. Think of it as the chill older cousin of the glitzier resorts.

Après-Ski Addict?

Hunter Mountain: Calling all party animals! Hunter Mountain is where the slopes meet the slopes... I mean, bars. With a lively après-ski scene that could rival Studio 54 in its heyday, this resort is perfect for those who like their evenings shaken (not stirred) with live music and good company. Just don't forget the ibuprofen for the morning.

Family Fun on the Slopes: ‍‍‍

Greek Peak Mountain Resort: Calling all Brady Bunch wannabes! Greek Peak is the perfect place for a multi-generational ski vacation. They've got a fantastic ski school for the little ones (and maybe some of the bigger ones who haven't quite mastered the snow plow), a water park for when the slopes get tiring, and enough activities to keep everyone entertained. Just avoid that awkward moment when grandma out-skis you.

The Underdog with Bite:

Belleayre Mountain: Don't let the smaller size fool you, Belleayre packs a punch. This recently renovated resort offers a surprising amount of terrain for all abilities, shorter lift lines (meaning more time carving up the slopes, less time channeling your inner yeti in the queue), and some of the most stunning scenery in the Catskills.

But Wait, There's More!

New York has a treasure trove of ski resorts, each with its own unique personality. From the challenging terrain of Whiteface Mountain to the beginner-friendly slopes of Bristol Mountain, there's a place for everyone. Just remember, the best resort is the one that leaves you with a smile on your face (and maybe a few bruises to brag about).

How-To FAQ for New York Ski Bums:

  • How to Get There: Most resorts are accessible by car. Some offer shuttle services or are near public transportation hubs.
  • How to Gear Up: Don't forget the essentials! Skis, boots, poles, warm clothes (layers are key!), helmet, sunscreen (yes, even on cloudy days!), and a sense of adventure.
  • How to Learn to Ski: Many resorts offer ski lessons for all ages and abilities.
  • How to Avoid Wipeouts: Take lessons, start on easier terrain, and don't be afraid to ask for help!
  • How to Après-Ski Like a Pro: Pace yourself on the slopes, hydrate, and refuel with delicious food and drinks. Remember, falling is inevitable, but falling flat on your face at the après-ski bar... well, let's just say that's optional.
