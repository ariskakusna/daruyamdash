How Many Airports In Los Angeles

Posted by Tandra Barner on Tuesday, August 20, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

Lost in LAX-land: How Many Airports Does Los Angeles Actually Need?

Ah, Los Angeles. The land of dreams, movie stars, and...apparently, a whole bunch of airports? You'd think navigating Hollywood's drama would be the toughest part of a trip to LA, but nope, it's just figuring out where your dang plane is even landing.

The Usual Suspects: The Big Five of LA Airfields

Los Angeles boasts a whopping five commercial airports. That's right, folks, enough runways to take you from the beaches of Santa Monica to, well, another beach in Orange County (looking at you, John Wayne Airport). Here's the lowdown on the main players:

  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): The big kahuna, the OG, the airport everyone knows and slightly dreads. LAX is a sprawling labyrinth that can hold more drama than a telenovela (seriously, check out the movie "Planes, Trains & Automobiles" for a glimpse).
  • Hollywood Burbank Airport (BUR): Perfect for those seeking a more intimate airport experience. Just don't be surprised if you bump into Ryan Reynolds while waiting for your baggage (it's practically in his backyard).
  • Long Beach Airport (LGB): This little guy might be the underdog, but it offers convenient access to the Port of Long Beach and those oh-so-tempting In-N-Out Burgers.
  • Ontario International Airport (ONT): Looking for a slightly cheaper flight? Ontario International might be your best bet. Just be prepared for a bit of a drive to get into the heart of LA (think of it as an extended Hollywood sightseeing tour...minus the stars).
  • John Wayne Airport (SNA): Technically not even in LA (it's in Orange County), but it serves the Greater Los Angeles area and is named after the Duke himself, so we're counting it.

So, Why So Many Airports, LA?

Is Los Angeles just really obsessed with airplanes? Maybe there's a secret competition between the cities to see who can build the most runways? The truth is, there are a few reasons for this abundance of airports:

  • Sprawl City, Sprawl Airports: Los Angeles is a massive city, and having multiple airports spread throughout the region helps alleviate traffic congestion (both on the ground and in the air).
  • Variety is the Spice of Air Travel: Different airports cater to different needs. LAX handles the bulk of international flights, while Burbank focuses on regional travel.

But Wait, There's More!

This isn't even the whole story. Los Angeles also has a bunch of smaller airports for private jets, cargo planes, and those daring souls who enjoy recreational flying (because apparently, that's a thing).

So, the next time you're planning a trip to LA, take a deep breath, grab a map, and maybe download a few extra travel apps. Just remember, with a little planning (and maybe a sense of humor), you'll navigate the LA airport scene like a pro. After all, even if you end up at the wrong airport, hey, at least you're still in sunny California!
