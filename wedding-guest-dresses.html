The best wedding-guest dresses to buy now and wear forever

Posted by Patria Henriques on Monday, August 19, 2024

We earn a commission for products purchased through some links in this article.

Sir

Australian brand Sir designs for modern women who want to be comfortable but look glamorous at the same time. The new season collection is full of beautiful dresses, most of which could easily win you 'best-dressed guest'.

Reformation

Credit: Courtesy

Yes, you absolutely can wear black to a wedding. For understated, fuss-free elegance, look no further than this Reformation design. Just accessorise with flare.

The Vampire's Wife

Bring the glamour in this shimmering design from Susie Cave's label, The Vampire's Wife. Perfect for a wedding with a smart dress code, it's made to be taken on the dancefloor.

Aje

Now 52% Off

Credit: Courtesy

Australian label Aje is known for creating pieces that imbue effortless beauty and tough femininity. While its elevated everyday looks are well worth the investment, we'd recommend its occasionwear for any weddings you have lined up.

Taller Marmo

Playful and vibrant, Taller Marmo's mini version of its cult, feathered Ubud dress will stand out for all the right reasons.

Zimmermann

Zimmermann delivers the prettiest of floral dresses, ready to solve your every sartorial wedding guest's need.

LoveShackFancy

Now 50% Off

Credit: Courtesy

For ultra-feminine, romantic designs, LoveShackFancy is a go-to destination. The brand's organza mini is appliquéd with blooming flowers, making it ideal for a spring or summer wedding.

Sleeper

Made from beautiful emerald green satin, this Sleeper dress features statement feathered cuffs (which are detachable) and a flattering maxi hem-length. It's perfect for a winter wedding that's sure to turn into a party.

Solace London

Sleek, voluminous and versatile, you can change the vibe of this statement Solace London mini with just the swap of accessories, meaning you'll get lots of different looks out of it in years to come.

Sister Jane

Every season, Sister Jane brings out a number of gems that just look so much more expensive than they are, and this red jacquard ruffled dress is only proof of that.
