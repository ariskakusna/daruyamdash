Can I Leave My Luggage At Boston Airport

Posted by Tandra Barner on Saturday, August 10, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

Stuck in Boston with Bags? Don't Be a Logan Lugger! ✈️

Let's face it, lugging your luggage around Boston like a lost puppy isn't exactly how you pictured conquering this historic city. Fear not, weary traveler! While Boston Logan Airport (affectionately nicknamed "Logan" by locals, because let's be honest, who has time for a full last name?) doesn't offer luggage storage lockers like some fancy-schmancy airports, there are still ways to ditch your bags and roam free!

Because Freedom Isn't Just an American Ideal, It's a Travel Necessity!

Here's the lowdown on how to become a luggage-less legend in Boston:

  • Befriend a Local Business: Think Airbnb, but for Bags! Companies like Stasher and Bounce partner with local shops, hotels, and cafes to create a network of luggage storage spots. Basically, you're turning a friendly barista into your temporary luggage concierge - how cool is that?

  • Get Stationed at South Station: This bustling transportation hub offers a couple of options. Greyhound Package Express and Teddy Ballgames are happy to hold your bags for a fee. Amtrak even has a baggage room, but that one's exclusive to ticket-holding train travelers. So, if you're rocking a backpack instead of a suitcase, this might not be your best bet.

  • Channel Your Inner Ocean Nomad: Okay, this one's a bit out there, but hear me out! The Rowes Wharf Water Taxi offers luggage storage for a price. While you might not be setting sail on the high seas, it could be a fun way to explore the Boston Harbor while your bags take a break.

Remember: Always check pricing and hours of operation before dropping your luggage off anywhere.

Now you're ready to roam like a luggage-less lobster! But before you dash off, here are some quick FAQs to make sure your smooth sailing continues:

How to find luggage storage locations near Logan Airport?

Easy! Search online for luggage storage services like Stasher or Bounce. They'll show you locations closest to the airport.

How much does luggage storage typically cost?

Prices can vary, but expect to pay around $5-$10 per bag, per day.

What if I'm only at the airport for a few hours?

Some storage options offer hourly rates, so you won't have to pay for a full day if you're short on time.

Are there any restrictions on what I can store?

Definitely! Most places won't accept perishable items, weapons, or anything else illegal. Always check with the storage provider beforehand.

How do I know my luggage will be safe?

Most reputable storage services offer insurance for your belongings. Check their website for details.

So there you have it! Now you can conquer Boston without being a Logan Lugger. Happy exploring!
